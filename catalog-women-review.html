💕 Diverse Women Personas Review

Ladies Nighty Design Catalog (Live Site Feedback)

8 Women from Different Ages, Backgrounds & Needs Review Their Real Experience

About This Review

This document captures feedback from 8 diverse women across Tamil Nadu who visited and browsed the Design Catalog website in real-time.

Each persona represents a unique shopping journey, need, and perspective. They reviewed the site's usability, product discovery, design appeal, and trust signals based on their actual browsing experience.

👩‍🎓

Ananya (College)

Age: 20 | Budget: ₹300–600 Tech: Very High | Language: English Shopping Style: Mobile-first, impulse buy
Journey: Opened on phone while scrolling Instagram → Scrolled through designs → Clicked like on 3 colorful floral options → Left without buying
♥️ What I Loved (3 points)
  • Instagram-style layout — Grid of cards with colorful photos feels like exploring a feed. Natural scrolling behavior. Love it
  • Like/Dislike voting — Makes me feel heard. I like rating products, gives me sense of control
  • Color dots at a glance — See instantly if my fave color is available. Saves scrolling through descriptions
✗ What Frustrated Me (4 points)
  • Can't filter by color! — I like the navy colorway. But I have to scroll through 40 cards to find navy in different designs. No color filter
  • No price info on card — Says "₹542 avg" but no "from ₹XXX to ₹YYY" range. Confusing for budget planning
  • Images don't zoom — On mobile, clicking image doesn't zoom or show detail. Hard to see fabric texture, stitching quality on small screen
  • No "Save" or "Add to Cart" — I want to bookmark 3 designs and come back to compare. But voting doesn't save anything for later
💬 Her Voice: "Design grid is giving main character energy, but I can't actually shop! No cart, no save, no color filter. Feels like window shopping, not real shopping."
👩‍💼

Priya (IT Professional)

Age: 27 | Budget: ₹1000–1500 Tech: Very High | Language: English-fluent Shopping Style: Research-driven, time-efficient
Journey: Visited from desktop → Looked for high-quality options → Checked ratings → Wanted to compare 3 finalists → Left frustrated (no detailed comparison)
♥️ What I Loved (3 points)
  • Rating display — Seeing ⭐⭐⭐⭐ 4.03 instantly tells me quality level. Professional touch
  • Responsive design — Works equally well on desktop and mobile. No janky behavior
  • Multi-angle product images — Showing 3 different product photos helps me judge design variations
✗ What Frustrated Me (4 points)
  • No fabric info at all — "₹542 avg" doesn't tell me if this is silk, cotton, or polyester. Quality indicator missing
  • No review excerpts or comments — Stars shown but no actual customer quotes. "What did buyers actually say?" — Can't tell
  • No way to compare designs side-by-side — If I want to pick between "Floral" and "Geometric," I have to switch back and forth mentally
  • Missing size/fit info — No mention of fit (loose, fitted, maxi). One design might be a flowy maxi, another a fitted short nighty. Can't tell
💬 Her Voice: "Looks polished, but it's all surface. No depth. I need comparison tools, fabric details, real reviews. This feels like a Pinterest board, not a shopping destination."
👩‍🍼

Divya (New Mom)

Age: 31 | Budget: ₹400–800 Tech: Medium | Language: Tamil + English Shopping Style: Task-focused, needs-driven
Journey: Searched specifically for "nursing nighty" → Got overwhelmed by all designs → Couldn't identify which are nursing-friendly → Gave up
♥️ What I Loved (2 points)
  • Half-sleeve & sleeveless options visible — Easy access is important for nursing. Seeing variety helps
  • Clean, simple interface — Not cluttered. Can browse quickly even while caring for baby (important!)
✗ What Frustrated Me (5 points)
  • NO NURSING-FRIENDLY FILTER/TAG — CRITICAL! Which designs have feeding zips? Which have front opening? NOT MARKED. I have to guess
  • No washability info — Will these stain easily with milk leaks? Are they machine washable? No care info provided
  • Comfort/fit descriptions vague — "Half Sleeve" tells me nothing about comfort for 12+ hours of wear (nursing moms wear these all day)
  • No size inclusivity notes — Do these fit after pregnancy body? Are they stretchy? No body-honest information
  • No testimonials from nursing moms — Would love to see: "I used this for 8 months, easy to nurse, washes great!" But nothing like that exists
💬 Her Voice: "Design catalog bagundhu, but nursing-friendly nu sollave illai! Naan guess pannalam na? I need to know which ones have zips, which have slits. This is too important to guess!"
👵

Saraswati (Retired)

Age: 62 | Budget: ₹200–400 Tech: Very Low | Language: Tamil-only Shopping Style: Guided by daughter, trust-based
Journey: Daughter opened site on tablet → Tried to understand descriptions (all English) → Saw colorful images → Felt overwhelmed → Asked daughter to decide
♥️ What I Loved (2 points)
  • Large product images — Even with old eyes, I can see the designs clearly
  • Simple, plain color options — White, green, navy are traditional, trustworthy colors for someone my age
✗ What Frustrated Me (6 points)
  • EVERYTHING IN ENGLISH — Descriptions, design names, all English. My daughter has to read and explain every card to me
  • No trusted brand names visible — I don't see "Bahumaan" or "G4Girl" or any familiar brands. Just generic descriptions. How do I know if it's quality?
  • Price displays confusing — "₹542 avg · 85 products" — What does this mean? ₹542 is the average I pay, or average seller price? Unclear
  • No shrinkage/durability info — I've been fooled before with cheap nighties that shrink after 3 washes. No quality guarantees mentioned
  • No return policy visible — If this doesn't fit or feel right, can I send it back? No information
  • Confusing voting system — Why am I being asked to "like" or "dislike" designs? I don't understand what I'm voting for or what happens with my vote
💬 Her Voice: "Magalin, yaar ipdi English-la solranga? Naan edhukku like-dislike pannanum? Trusted brand nu sollave illai. Digital payment-ku verumbikale, sathiyam kuralai!"
(Daughter, why is everything in English? Why do I have to vote? No trusted brand names. I'm worried about digital payment!)
👗

Isha (Plus-Size)

Age: 35 | Budget: ₹800–1500 Tech: High | Language: English-fluent Shopping Style: Determined but frustrated
Journey: Browsed all 40+ designs → Wondered "Do these come in my size?" → Realized no size info provided → Felt excluded → Closed tab
♥️ What I Loved (1 point)
  • Variety of designs shown — At least there are options. Some sites don't even try to show plus-friendly styles
✗ What Frustrated Me (6 points)
  • ZERO SIZE INFORMATION — Do these come in XXL? XXXL? Are they one-size-fits-all? Completely unclear. Feels like I'm not the target customer
  • No plus-size specific filtering or tags — Can't search "plus-size friendly" or see which designs have generous fits
  • No body-honest reviews — Would love to see reviews from women my size saying "This actually fit me!" But nothing like that
  • Models in images appear standard size — Can I see how this looks on someone with a body like mine? Not shown
  • No size-inclusive language — "Free Size" mentioned sometimes, but no explanation. Does "Free" mean XXL-friendly or just one size?
  • Pricing same as standard sizes — If I pay more for plus-size fabric, I should know upfront. No transparency
💬 Her Voice: "I spend 30% more than standard-size shoppers, but this site pretends I don't exist. No size info, no plus-friendly reviews, no body diversity. I shop elsewhere where they actually want my business."
👰

Meera (Bride)

Age: 26 | Budget: ₹1500–3000 Tech: Medium | Language: Tamil + English Shopping Style: Intentional, quality-focused
Journey: Searched for wedding/trousseau nighties → Looked for premium fabrics (silk, satin) → Wanted quality assurance → Found no bridal-specific curation
♥️ What I Loved (3 points)
  • Elegant design variety — Lace, embroidery options present. Some designs definitely feel "special occasion" worthy
  • Color options for different preferences — Peach, cream, burgundy, pink — romantic colors for newlyweds
  • High ratings (4.0+) — Customer satisfaction shows. Important for wedding purchases
✗ What Frustrated Me (4 points)
  • No fabric quality hierarchy — Which is pure silk? Which is polyester satin? No distinction between ₹500 satin vs. ₹2000 premium silk
  • No "Trousseau" or "Wedding" category — I have to guess which designs are suitable for my wedding night. No curation for this special occasion
  • No durability or care guarantees — For a ₹2000 nighty, I want to know: Will it last 2 years? How do I care for it? No information
  • Missing luxury signals — Brand names, designer info, heritage story. Nothing that justifies premium pricing or adds confidence in quality
💬 Her Voice: "Beautiful designs, but no confidence this is actually premium quality. For my wedding, I want something that feels special and lasts. This catalog doesn't convince me of either."

Lakshmi (Working Mom)

Age: 42 | Budget: ₹400–700 Tech: Low-Medium | Language: Tamil-comfortable Shopping Style: Practical, time-limited
Journey: Quick 2-minute browse on phone → Wanted full-length designs → Noticed 3-quarter sleeve option → Wondered if available → Browsed quickly, left without engaging
♥️ What I Loved (2 points)
  • Sleeve length variety visible — Half, three-quarter, sleeveless shown. Easy to find what I prefer without descriptions
  • Quick visual scanning — I have 2 minutes to browse. Grid layout helps me scan quickly without reading long text
✗ What Frustrated Me (4 points)
  • No fabric wash-care summary — As a working mom with laundry to do, I need quick care info. "Machine wash? Hand wash? Permanent press?" Not mentioned
  • No COD option visible — I don't trust online payments yet. Do you accept Cash on Delivery? Not shown on this page
  • No bulk pricing for combo buys — If I want to buy 3 nighties (one for home, one for office stay, one backup), is there a bulk discount? No info
  • Voting system confuses me — Why am I clicking like/dislike? Does it help me shop faster next time? No clarity on purpose
💬 Her Voice: "Nice designs, but I work full-time, come home exhausted. I need practical info: wash care, COD option, bulk deals. This catalog doesn't speak my language. I shop with my phone in one hand, laundry basket in the other!"

Neha (Eco-Conscious)

Age: 28 | Budget: ₹800–1500 Tech: Very High | Language: English-fluent Shopping Style: Values-driven, sustainable
Journey: Opened catalog looking for sustainable options → No eco-badges or labels visible → Looked for organic cotton indicators → Left disappointed
♥️ What I Loved (1 point)
  • Cotton options available — At least there are cotton designs. Better than all-synthetic fast fashion
✗ What Frustrated Me (5 points)
  • No sustainability information — Is the cotton organic? Fair-trade? Sustainably sourced? Zero transparency on environmental impact
  • No ethical sourcing badges — Are these made in fair-wage factories? No certifications or labels mentioned
  • No durability/longevity messaging — Sustainable fashion is about buying less, wearing longer. But no "Built to Last" positioning
  • No brand transparency — Which companies make these? Can I verify their sustainability claims? Anonymous designs don't inspire trust
  • No take-back/recycling info — If I buy this, can I recycle it when worn? No circular economy option mentioned
💬 Her Voice: "I'm willing to pay more for sustainable products, but this catalog doesn't market sustainability at all. No eco-badges, no brand stories, no impact data. How do I know if I'm buying ethical fashion or greenwashing?"

⚡ Quick UX Wins (Can Implement This Week)

  • Add "Fabric Type" badge on each card (Cotton, Silk, Satin, Polyester) in simple colored tag
  • Add "Nursing-Friendly ✓" label for designs with feeding zips/access
  • Show price range instead of "avg" (₹400–600 instead of ₹542 avg)
  • Add "Size Available" note (e.g., "Available: M, L, XL, Free Size")
  • Convert voting to "Save Design" button so users can actually track favorites across sessions
  • Add 1–2 customer review snippets on each card (short quote + rating)
  • Add bilingual toggle (English ↔ Tamil) in header
  • Show "Care:" note (Machine Wash, Avoid Bleach, etc.) in small text

This diverse persona feedback reveals a critical gap: The catalog is visually beautiful but functionally incomplete for real shopping.

Women are voting on designs they love, but they can't actually buy them. They're excluded by missing size info, language barriers, and lack of product transparency. The site feels like inspiration (Pinterest) not commerce (Flipkart).
